Home Work Details

Background

The SPFM website design project was initiated to provide a comprehensive platform that reflects the values and services of Sunway Property & Facility Management (SPFM). The website serves as a complimentary tool for the organization, showcasing their offerings and enhancing client engagement. In collaboration with IMTTECH SDN BHD, the project also highlights detailed information about SPFM's services and their commitment to excellence in property management.

High-level Goal

The goal of the SPFM website design is to create a responsive platform that delivers a consistent user experience across all devices, aligning with SPFM’s core principles. The website aims to attract potential clients and customers by showcasing available properties, providing transparent information on property values, and offering insights into the SPFM organization and its services.

My Impact

My role in the project involved collaborating on the responsive website design, utilizing assets from the illustrator, and following guidance from the Board. I adhered to SPFM’s brand guidelines and iterated based on feedback received during weekly meetings. I focused on ensuring the website design became an essential touchpoint for attracting potential clients and customers, contributing to the sale of Sunway properties through an engaging and informative platform.

Research

Goals

Below are the areas I’d like to explore during the user research. I want to understand:
  • Understand user needs to create a seamless website experience for SPFM clients and potential buyers.
  • Identify pain points in accessing property information to enhance usability.
  • Ensure the website effectively communicates SPFM's offerings and organizational values.

Methodologies

  • Analyze user behavior through surveys and feedback forms to understand the board’s expectations.
  • Study similar industry websites to identify key features and best practices.
  • Conduct usability testing to refine the website’s functionality and user flow.

Ideation

Site Map

The first step in the ideation phase was to create a clear and organized site map for the SPFM website. This map outlines the core structure, defining the key sections such as property listings, service offerings, company information, and client resources. The goal was to ensure intuitive navigation for potential clients and visitors, allowing them to access important information about the organization, property values, and available services seamlessly. A well-defined site map helps streamline the user journey, providing clarity and ease of use across all devices.

Task & User Flows

I created the user task flow for visitors to view the available list of properties or check the pricing of houses. The task flow assumes that the visitor is actively searching for a property, ensuring a seamless process that guides them through exploring listings and pricing details, making it easy for potential clients to find relevant information quickly and efficiently.

Lo-fi Wireframes

After defining the primary user tasks and flows, I created the initial set of low-fidelity wireframes to conduct early testing. This approach helped gather initial feedback and allowed me to address usability issues early, streamlining the process before moving on to the high-fidelity prototype.

Following the first round of testing, I made several iterations, including:

  • Adjusting the layout to enhance navigation through property listings.
  • Simplifying the search functionality for quicker access to pricing details.
  • Optimizing the display of property images to improve user engagement.

Test

To gain valuable insight and feedback on the branding and usability, I tested the prototype with 5 potential users (ages 31-39). This testing provided essential input, allowing me to refine and implement changes for the second round of iterations. The test achieved a 100% successful completion rate, indicating the design's effectiveness and user-friendly approach.

User test revealed a list of priority revisions that I implemented to the high-fidelity prototype 1 that included:
  • Streamlined the navigation for a more intuitive browsing experience.
  • Optimized the layout of property information for clearer presentation.
  • Enhanced the search functionality to filter by price, location, and property type.

Conclusion

Through the synthesis of research results, I discovered significant discrepancies between user behaviors and needs and the offerings SPFM currently presents. Given the limited timeline to deliver the final product, I prioritized action items that align closely with SPFM’s services and goals. These recommendations are structured in a way that allows for gradual implementation, ensuring that the website can evolve over time while addressing both user expectations and SPFM's strategic objectives.
